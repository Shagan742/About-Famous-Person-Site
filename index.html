Fun Facts

This legend is basically saying "bloody Mary" 3 times in front of a mirror with candles lit around it to "summon" her. This legend was named after Mary I being called "bloody Mary" due to burning a ton of people to stake.

Mary I was considered illegitimate because her father wanted to divorce her mother, but the Church of England did not allow it because of Mary's existence. So, to deny her existence and be able to divorce, King Henry VIII considered her illegitimate.

Languages Mary I was fluent in were Latin, Spanish, and French. She could read and write in them since early childhood.

Queen Elizabeth I was born from King Henry VIII and Mary I's first step-mother, Anne Boleyn. She counts more as a half sister of Mary's. Their age gap is 17 years, meaning Mary I was 17 years old when Elizabeth I was born.

After Mary I died in 1558, Elizabeth I took the throne, replacing her. This was according to Henry VIII's will, which Mary I was reluctant to follow.
